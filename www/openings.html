About me

Research topics

My research goal is to develop optimization and learning theory for multi-objective and meta learning, especially under overparameterized settings with limited labeled data supervision, in order to better explain and predict the behavior of existing algorithms, to guide the design of principled, scalable and generalizable algorithms, and to apply to large-scale machine learning and signal processing problems.

Topics include but are not limited to:

  • Optimization: convergence analysis and scalable algorithm design for multi-objective and multi-level optimization (nonconvex, constrained, stochastic);

  • Learning theory for multi-objective/task/modal learning and meta learning (algorithm-dependent bounds, high-dimensional statistics, overparameterized / few-shot settings);

  • Applications to speech processing, computer vision, foundation model finetuning / alignment, multi-modal / distributed systems, trustworthy AI.

About University of Rochester

The University of Rochester (UR) is a private, Tier I research university that consistently ranks among the top 30 institutions, both public and private, in federal funding for research and development. UR has superb facilities and multiple interdisciplinary research centers such as the Goergen Institute for Data Science, the Center for Biomedical Ultrasound, and the Center for Visual Science. UR is at the heart of the high-tech Finger Lakes Region of New York State, providing many opportunities for industry collaboration as well as a high quality of life.
